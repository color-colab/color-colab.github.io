The infrastructure behind ColorColab

REQUIREMENTS

Hardware

ColorColab will require three main pieces of hardware: an iPad, a Magneo “magnifying glass” augmented reality interface, and a steel stand.

The stand is to be placed 0.5 meters away from the statue — close enough to signal to visitors it is related to this artifact specifically, but also far enough to let users stand between them to explore the statue itself. The stand will not be screwed into the ground, as it will be made of steel and thus heavy enough to not be knocked over.

The iPad and the Magneo will be available in a stand next to the sculpture to be explored. While the Magneo simply lies on one of the shelfs of the stand, the iPad will be connected on its bottom to a charger on the stand. The iPad and the magnifying glass will not be phsyically ligared to the stand. Rather, they will have a chip system that will ring if the user takes them more than 3 meters away from the stand (and the user will be warned about this in the stand itself —“Don’t take me too far away from my home”). The iPad will not have a passcode, so the user can immediately access it.

Unlike other AR systems, which use head-mounted or head-up displays, the "magnifying glass" approach—a type of Handheld Augmented Reality—employs a palm-sized video see-through device to present computer augmented view of the real world. This configuration's advantages over traditional configurations include that the user doesn't have to put on any cumbersome head gear. Like a magnifying glass, the user can easily move the device around and compare real and augmented images.

Furthermore, the choice to utilize a separate interface for the AR (as opposed to using the iPad and its built in camera for the AR interface) comes from the goal of sparking curiosity in the users in an intuitive, almost child-like way for users of all ages. The shape of a magnifying glass is intuitive for almost any person, regardles of age or social background. 

The Magneo device was specifically designed for children with its unique round-shaped screen, which enables children-focused exploratory learning. Its minimalist design enables learning and fosters curiosity in children and adults, as it makes exploration fun dn interactive and stimulates creativity. Magneo has achieved numerous Global awards, including the German iF 2015 Design Award, the Italian A’Design Award, the 2017 US CES Annual Innovation Award, and the 2017 TopDigital Awards.

Digital Data

The most basic needed data underlying the ColorColab will be a series of image-based 3d models of sculptures, with an additional layer of appliable texture that is customizable.These 3d models will, as the rest of the required digital data, live in the ColorColab web server, which is accessed by the Unity application.

A relational database framework will be employed to store the color palette and texture database. In its most basic form, a ground "color" database will be a lower layer with three columns: HEX color code, RGB color code and a pointer to an image square of the color. Each of the HEX codes may have additional textual notes, as for example if it is representative of an ancient pigment as supported by scholarly research. Additionally, the database will be enriched with user-inputted pictures and color tones, which will have additional textual notes.

This basic database of colors will be the basis of another database surrounding color palettes. This database will simply be a list of pointers to the color database. Each palette will also have textual a pointer to a textual database with descriptions and metadata of the palettes if they are pre-defined, and with user notes if they are user-uploaded.
