Data Gathering / Research 

Data on the traction of the 2017+ exposé articles 

How interested are people in these issues? Who are the interested parties? Has there been backlash? If so, by who, and why? 


Data on attempts at exposing polychromy in direct interaction to the material sculptures 

Gods in Color 2018 exhibition - material renderings on copies of the sculptures 

Easiest, and cheapest, way for museums to address the fact of polychromy is to say more about it in their labelling 

Has there been any attempt at using modern tech for projected reconstruction? 


Sculpture Research: sculptures with evidence of polychromy, and available color hypothesis 

What are some examples of sculptures that have been proven to have been polychromous? 

What do the colored hypothesis look like? What are the ranges of color? Do they show mostly people? If so, do they show different skin colors?  


Location-based Research: Italy 

Are any of these classical sculptures of interest  (or copies of them) in Rome or Bologna? If not, are there other classical sculptures that might have been polychromos and simply not researched? Are any of these classical sculptures in Italy? Where? Who owns them? How accessible are they?  

Is there an Italian interest in teaching the general public about polychromy? If so, by who (organizations, universities, museums, collections)?  


Methods research: the use of AR for reconstructive projects 

What have been the uses of AR within museums to interact with sculptural artifacts? What about outside museums? 

Have AR technologies been used in public spaces, for public interventions? 

What are the benefits of using AR technology for reconstructive purposes? 

What are the challenges? 


Implementation research 

What are the technologies needed? 

What kind of working group is needed? Ideally art historians, technologists, digital humanists, museum curators, computer scientists, archeologists, etc? 

What is the budget of this kind of project? 

What is the best starting point for a prototype?

Step 2: Palette creation  

Palettes Type 1: Classical Hues 

Description: Colors/Pigments/Hues used by Greek/Roman Painters in Classical Antiquity of Ancient Greece and Rome. 

Source:http://www.visual-arts-cork.com/artist-paints/classical-colour-palette.htm 

Example Hues: 

Reds: Realgar, Dragons Blood, Vermilion (Minium) 

Purples: Indigo, Madder, Woad, Tyrian purple 

Blues: Azurite, Egyptian Blue (Frit), Ultramarine (Lazuline Blue) 

Greens: Verdigris, Terre Verte, Stone Green, Verdetta, and Celadonite, Egyptian Green, Malachite (Mineral Green or Verdeazzuro). 

Yellows: Massicot, Naples Yellow (Giallorino), Orpiment. 

Browns: Sienna, Umber. 

Whites: Lead White, Gypsum, Chalk. 

Blacks: Lamp Black, Carbon Black (charcoal), new Ivory Black. 


Palettes Type 2: Classical Stamps and Prints 

Description: Prints/Designs used by Greek/Roman Painters in Classical Antiquity of Ancient Greece and Rome (including in a variety of surfaces— clothes, frescoes, buildings, objects). 

Examples:  

Harlequin pattern (From Trojan archer in Gods in Color) 

Palettes Type 3: Modern Hues 

Description: Artist Colors/Pigments/Hues/Trends most popular in the last five years within the graphic design, high fashion, and advertising worlds. Pantone codes. 

Examples: 

Rose Quartz and Serenity (colors of the year in 2016) 

Metallics as neutrals 

Bright and bold 


Palettes Type 4: Modern Stamps and Prints
